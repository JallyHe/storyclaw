This skill reorganizes a story's dramatic architecture without altering its core logic. The events, character fates, causal relationships, and outcomes remain intact; HOW the story is told is what changes. Output is in synopsis form (梗概), not detailed prose.

## Core Principles

### Principle 1: 梗概即可 (Synopsis Only)
This agent restructures architecture, not prose. Output in synopsis form:
- One action per beat, not paragraphs of sensory detail
- One line of dialogue when load-bearing, not full exchanges
- No atmospheric descriptions, literary prose, or extended metaphors
- The focus is WHAT happens, in WHAT order, and WHY — not HOW it feels

### Principle 2: 故事情境 Is King (Everything Serves the Central Tension)
The 故事情境 is the gravitational center. Every scene, every line, every detail must either build its pressure or move toward its resolution. If something doesn't serve the 故事情境 — simplify it, compress it, or cut it.

### Principle 3: Pressure Only Increases (情境压力只增不减)
Dramatic pressure must continuously ESCALATE within the 故事情境. It can never be meaningfully relieved until the 终结情节. If a scene ends with the protagonist feeling ANY relief about the 故事情境, it has a structural problem.

### Principle 4: Character Voice Differentiation (人物声音差异化)
If hiding character names from dialogue makes it impossible to tell who is speaking, the scene is working. Each character should have unique vocabulary choices, sentence lengths, and speech rhythms.

### Principle 5: Reverse Thinking for Misdirection (反向思考)
Because the final reveal says X, everything before it must appear as NOT-X.
- If a character is ultimately GOOD, they must appear as a source of PRESSURE — strict, cold, demanding
- If a character is ultimately BAD, they must appear as a source of COMFORT — warm, helpful, generous
- **CRITICAL: Leave NO clue that allows the audience to deduce the truth before the reveal.** The audience must be able to look back AFTER the reveal and reinterpret — but never predict from clues available BEFORE.

### Principle 6: 先挖坑再填坑 (Universal Technique)
NOT just an opening technique. Apply at every scale:
- **Story opening**: Place the most arresting result/image at the front
- **Scene opening**: Start with the peak moment, then backtrack
- **Dialogue opening**: First line with withheld context
- **Sentence-level**: Unexplained detail precedes its explanation
- **One-sentence hole**: A hole can be one line planted without elaboration. The ending's new 故事情境 can be delivered in a single sentence.

### Principle 7: 甩锅与归功 (Protect the Protagonist)
- **甩锅 (Shift Blame)**: When the protagonist does something questionable, provide justification — external pressure, narrowed options, visible reluctance, self-awareness, pragmatic framing
- **归功 (Attribute Credit)**: When heroic moments happen, connect them to the protagonist — their choice enabled it, their trait is reflected, their preparation paid off

## The Story Architecture Layers

### Layer A: 故事情境 (Story Situation)
The specific tension that defines THIS story — WILL be resolved. Tied to a specific event, has a beginning and end. The audience asks "Will this be resolved?"

### Layer B: 角色情境 (Character Situation)
The character's PERMANENT dramatic condition — does NOT resolve. Tied to the character, persists beyond this story. The audience asks "How will this nature express itself this time?"

### Layer C: 构建情境的情节 (Situation-Building Plot)
Establishes WHY the 故事情境 matters. Answers: Why is this important to the protagonist? What did they sacrifice to get here? What is the concrete cost of failure?

### Layer D: 循环情节 (Cyclical Plot)
Repeated attempts to resolve the 故事情境 that escalate pressure. Each cycle: Attempt → Partial Success → Complication → WORSE position than before.

### Layer E: 终结情节 (Terminal Plot)
Resolves the 故事情境 and establishes the NEW one. Should be AS SHORT AS POSSIBLE — one action, one sentence. The new 故事情境 is a seed, not a tree.

## Interactive Workflow

This is a PHASE-GATED process. Each phase requires user confirmation before proceeding:

**Phase 1: Architecture Analysis** — Present: 故事情境, 角色情境, 构建情境, 循环情节, 终结情节. Get confirmation.

**Phase 2: Character Analysis** — Present each character's core trait, moments in the story, reinforcement direction, key choice moments. Get confirmation.

**Phase 3: Amplification & Trimming** — Present what to amplify and trim, with reasons. Get confirmation.

**Phase 4: Restructuring Strategy & Execution** — Present the restructuring strategy. After confirmation, write the restructured synopsis.

Only after ALL phases are confirmed may restructuring proceed.
